AED (Automated External Defibrillator) Module

Learn the step-by-step process of using an AED in an emergency.

Scenario: An adult collapses in the shopping mall. They are unresponsive and not breathing normally. A nearby store employee retrieves an AED from the wall. You are trained in CPR and arrive first.

Step-by-Step AED Deployment

  1. Turn ON the AED - Look for the ON/OFF button or lid
  2. Expose the Patient's Chest - Remove clothing; remove jewelry and medication patches
  3. Dry the Chest - If wet, dry with a towel
  4. Place Adhesive Pads - First pad: upper right chest (below collarbone). Second pad: left side mid-axillary line at 5th-6th rib
  5. Connect Cables - Plug pads into AED if not pre-connected
  6. Follow Voice Prompts - The AED will analyze the heart rhythm
  7. Deliver Shock if Advised - Keep clear of patient, press SHOCK button when prompted
  8. Resume CPR - After shock, immediately resume chest compressions

Recognizing & Responding to Shock

Shock is a life-threatening condition where the body is not receiving enough oxygenated blood. Learn to recognize and treat it.

Signs & Symptoms of Shock

  • Pale, cold, clammy skin
  • Rapid, weak pulse
  • Rapid, shallow breathing
  • Weakness or confusion
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Dilated pupils
  • Loss of consciousness

Treatment for Shock

  1. Call 911 immediately
  2. Lay the patient flat (unless spinal injury is suspected)
  3. Elevate legs 12 inches (if no spinal injury)
  4. Keep patient warm with blankets
  5. Do NOT give food or water
  6. Reassure and monitor consciousness
  7. Be ready to perform CPR
